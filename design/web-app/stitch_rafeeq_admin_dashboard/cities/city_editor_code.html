Cairo, Egypt's sprawling capital, is set on the Nile River. At its heart is Tahrir Square and the vast Egyptian Museum, a trove of antiquities including royal mummies and gilded King Tutankhamun artifacts.

The City of a Thousand Minarets

Nearby, Giza is the site of the iconic pyramids and Great Sphinx, dating to the 26th century BC. In Gezira Island’s leafy Zamalek district, the Cairo Tower provides panoramic city views. The historic Old Cairo is a labyrinth of alleyways, churches, and bustling bazaars like Khan el-Khalili.

Ideal for cultural tourism, Cairo serves as the primary gateway for all international travelers entering Egypt.
